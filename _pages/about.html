Welcome! I'm Jared Cregg, a postdoc at the University of Copenhagen.

My research in neuroscience focuses on understanding the functional organization of brainstem motor circuits. Behavior is characteristically complex, yet ultimately manifests as specific motor instructions within output circuitries of the spinal cord. Uniquely, the brainstem serves as a vital conduit through which information passes from higher-order circuits onto ‘command lines’ that relay these instructions to spinal motor networks. Thus, the brainstem is a crucial bottleneck for sensorimotor processing.

How do higher-order networks interface with brainstem command lines to execute specific behaviors? To address this question, I am using optogenetics, calcium imaging, electrophysiology, and cell-type- and projection-specific viral tracing to reveal brainstem motor infrastructure in sophisticated anatomical, molecular, and functional detail. My research is supported by postdoctoral fellowships from the European Molecular Biology Organization (EMBO) and the Lundbeck Foundation.
